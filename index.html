The Garden Plan

Tap a bed to see its plants — a Cape Cod native & pollinator palette you can swap and rearrange.

Seven beds around the house. Each one opens a planting tray below where you can move plants by dragging, swap any plant for another, and add or remove them. The palette leans on Cape Cod native, drought- and salt-tolerant, pollinator-friendly species — “right plant, right place” — so the garden feeds bees, butterflies, and birds while protecting groundwater and the bays. Each bed is arranged by classic design rules: plants are grouped in odd-numbered drifts (the Rule of Three: 3, 5, or 7 — never lonely singles), those drifts repeat across the bed for cohesion, and varied foliage textures are mixed so a bed reads well even out of bloom.
